A Trading 212 portfolio tracker is a third-party tool that analyses your investment data beyond what the Trading 212 app provides. Trading 212 shows you live prices and basic account value, but it doesn't break down your dividend income, calculate your true yield on cost, or show you when you trade most. External trackers fill that gap by importing your CSV export and surfacing deeper insights.

---

## Why Trading 212's Built-In Analytics Fall Short

Trading 212 has grown rapidly, the platform now serves over 4.5 million funded accounts with more than £6.6 billion in client assets (Trading 212, 2024). But its analytics tools have not kept pace with that growth.

What Trading 212 shows you:
- Current portfolio value
- Unrealised gain/loss per holding
- Basic transaction history

What Trading 212 doesn't show you:
- Total dividend income over time
- Yield on cost (dividend income relative to what you paid)
- P&L broken down by realised vs unrealised
- Trading patterns and activity heatmaps
- Performance vs benchmark (e.g. FTSE 100 or S&P 500)
- Multi-year return calculations

For a passive investor holding dividend stocks or ETFs, this missing data matters. Knowing your yield on cost, your actual total return including dividends, and whether your income is growing year-on-year is fundamental to making informed decisions.

**Best for:** Trading 212 investors who want dividend and yield-on-cost analysis without creating an account.

## Portseido

**Best for:** Multi-broker investors who want a clean interface and solid P&L reporting.

Portseido supports multiple brokers and manual entry alongside CSV imports. The free tier is reasonable for casual tracking. Paid plans unlock more accounts and advanced features. It does not calculate yield on cost, so for dividend-focused investors it's less useful than Vizvest for that specific metric.

---

## Sharesight

**Best for:** Investors who need formal tax reporting.

Sharesight is the most established name in UK portfolio tracking. It calculates CGT reports and dividend income statements suitable for HMRC self-assessment. It's overkill for casual tracking, and the paid plans are expensive (starting around £18/month). Trading 212 imports are supported via CSV.

---

## Simple Portfolio

**Best for:** Long-term investors who want clean performance charts.

Simple Portfolio focuses on total return performance over time. The interface is minimal and easy to use. Tax reporting is available on paid plans. Less dividend-focused than Vizvest but good for seeing how your overall portfolio performs vs a benchmark.

---

## PinkLion

**Best for:** Investors who want AI-powered portfolio insights.

PinkLion includes AI tools for portfolio optimisation and stress testing using historical market events (e.g. "how would my portfolio have performed during the 2020 crash?"). At ~€5/month it's affordable, but it's a paid tool where Vizvest covers the core dividend and P&L analysis for free.

---

## AllInvestView

**Best for:** Investors who want automatic syncing via API.

AllInvestView connects to Trading 212 via SnapTrade for automatic trade imports rather than manual CSV uploads. This is convenient if you want live data without re-exporting regularly. The free tier is generous. Less focused on dividend metrics than Vizvest.

## Feature-by-Feature Breakdown

### Dividend Tracking

Most trackers show total dividend income. Vizvest goes further by showing yield on cost, the dividend return relative to what you originally paid, not the current price. For long-term dividend investors, yield on cost is a more meaningful metric than current dividend yield. An investor who bought Unilever at £30 years ago and receives £1.50/share in dividends has a 5% yield on cost, regardless of where the stock trades today.

### Privacy

Tools that require account creation store your financial data on their servers. If that concerns you, Vizvest processes your CSV locally in the browser, your data stays on your device.

### P&L Accuracy

Trading 212's built-in P&L only shows unrealised gains (what you'd make if you sold today). It doesn't clearly show realised gains from completed trades, and it can be distorted by currency movements. Dedicated trackers calculate both realised and unrealised P&L separately and handle multi-currency portfolios more transparently.

### Tax Reporting

None of the free tools listed above produce HMRC-ready CGT reports. For tax reporting, Sharesight's paid plans or a specialist CGT calculator are better options.
